# AGENTIC MESH ANALYSIS
# Seasonal Risk Report

## Executive Summary
Analysis of recent operational data reveals a critical correlation between ambient temperature and energy consumption. 

## Key Findings
- **Temperature Threshold:** Operations at ambient temperatures below 25°C maintain a steady energy consumption (avg. 447.5 KWh).
- **Impact of High Heat:** Once temperatures exceed 30°C (specifically observed at 34°C), energy consumption spiked to 890 KWh—a **99.3% increase**.
- **Efficiency Ratio Projection:** Assuming production levels remain constant, the Energy Efficiency Ratio (KWh per unit produced) is projected to degrade by nearly 50% if temperatures remain above 30°C.

## Risk Assessment
The doubling of energy requirements during high-heat periods poses a significant risk to operational margins and sustainability targets. Cooling systems should be inspected for efficiency, and peak-shaving strategies should be considered for high-temperature forecasts.
